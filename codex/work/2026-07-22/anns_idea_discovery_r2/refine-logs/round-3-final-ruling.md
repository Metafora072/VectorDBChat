# Refinement Final Ruling

## Status

**NOT READY / HOLD-RETHINK.** No proposal reached the refinement target. The problem survives; the proposed certificate mechanism does not.

## What is retained

- Formal objective: exact-reference terminal/cumulative divergence under a total ANN effort budget.
- Causal decomposition: direct local ANN error, pure query-state drift, and end-to-end trajectory error.
- Empirical signal: the SIFT100K pilot warrants a stronger phenomenon test.
- Evaluation discipline: matched local recall/hardness, equal wall-clock, and oracle gap.

## What is killed

- A frontier-alternative certificate on ordinary HNSW/Vamana.
- Any theorem that treats the hard top-k transition as globally Lipschitz.
- A controller that assumes an unobserved next-block risk reduction.
- The claim that a generic dynamic-`ef` wrapper is a paper contribution.

## Only permissible next A0

Before full implementation, run a small **mechanism pre-gate** for Route A:

1. At geometric effort checkpoints, log result-summary displacement `z_l` and the eventual exact-local summary error.
2. Test whether the recent displacement history predicts the residual error after controlling for top-k set stability, margin, visited nodes, local recall, and a DARTH-style hardness score.
3. Charge every extra checkpoint and summary update to wall-clock.
4. Continue only if the result-change signal produces a large incremental effect and an equal-time stopping policy beats ordinary patience/margin/hardness baselines by at least 25% on two datasets.

This A0 validates only whether a publishable mechanism may exist. It does not revive the certificate claim. Failure is `KILL-DYNAMIC-EF`.

Route B (bounded unseen regions) requires a separate novelty review and a high-dimensional bound-tightness pilot. It is not silently merged into TraceGuard.
